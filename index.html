01

Introduction — Why this platform exists

Many women quietly delay the decision to begin therapy. Not because they are unwilling, but because the path toward the right therapist is often unclear, impersonal, and — when presented through existing digital platforms — more transactional than therapeutic.

This platform exists to change that first experience. It is a women-centered digital environment, designed with clinical input from the first line of code, to make the early steps of seeking support feel safer, simpler and more human.

We are not building a marketplace. We are building a quiet, curated space where women can begin.

02

The problem — why current platforms fall short

Most existing digital therapy platforms are structured around volume. They treat therapist selection as a filtering problem, clinical information as marketing copy, and the first visit as a conversion funnel.

The result is a category of products that look efficient but feel cold. Users report hesitation, decision fatigue, and a lack of emotional orientation before they have even spoken to a professional. For many women — especially those approaching therapy for the first time — this is enough friction to delay seeking support for months or years.

There is also a quieter issue: a lack of editorial discipline. Content is often generic, clinically imprecise, or written to generate traffic rather than to support understanding. Few platforms take clinical tone seriously at the product layer.

03

Our thesis — therapy should feel safe from the first click

Trust is not a feature. It is the entire product.

Our thesis is that every interaction — the first landing page, the intake questions, the content someone reads while still deciding — is part of the therapeutic experience. Each of these moments either lowers the threshold to seek help, or quietly raises it.

We design with that understanding. The platform is built so a visitor can arrive in a difficult moment, read carefully, breathe, and proceed only when ready. Nothing is urgent. Nothing is engineered to pressure a decision.

04

What makes this different

Clinically authored

Intake questions, editorial voice, content library and the therapist-led Q&A are all shaped by practitioners, not by marketing logic.

Women-centered by design

Not a demographic label. Reflected in the therapists we curate, the topics we prioritise, the language we use, and the emotional pacing of the product.

Deliberately small

The MVP focuses on doing one thing exceptionally well: helping a woman find a therapist she trusts, and beginning her therapy with clarity.

05

Built by clinicians, not technologists alone

The intellectual and ethical foundation of the platform is held by two licensed mental health professionals.

Lisa and Johanna do not sit on an advisory board. They are the editorial authority of the platform. They define clinical tone, select therapists, shape the content library, and set the standards that the technical decisions must serve. This is what we mean when we say the platform is authored, not simply built.

06

The digital foundation — human-centered technology

The clinical vision is matched by a disciplined, experienced digital team from xophus.

The team has deliberately chosen a lean, AI-assisted engineering approach. Artificial intelligence is used carefully — as an internal tool to accelerate development, and, in future, as a gentle assistant within the product. It is not positioned as a decision-maker, a therapist, or a screening authority. Those roles remain human.

08

Market entry and controlled growth strategy

The MVP will launch within three to four months, targeting English- and Greek-speaking women in Europe, on a privacy-first, GDPR-aligned infrastructure hosted in the EU.

We intend to grow slowly and intentionally. The first months will focus on a small, curated network of therapists, a focused editorial programme, and direct engagement with women who trust Lisa and Johanna's clinical voices. Growth beyond the MVP — group sessions, therapist messaging, subscription models, mobile, AI-assisted matching — will only follow clear signal from real users.

This is a category where early scale is the enemy of trust. We plan accordingly.

09

Why we believe this matters now

The demand for accessible, credible mental health support continues to rise, particularly among women navigating life transitions, caregiving responsibilities, neurodiversity and recovery from trauma. At the same time, trust in fast-moving, AI-led mental health products is — appropriately — under scrutiny.

There is a clear space for a platform that moves carefully, writes clearly, respects privacy, and is authored by clinicians. We believe this space is best entered now, before the category consolidates around volume-driven models.

10

A responsible first step

This is a deliberate, small and serious initiative. Four experienced people — two clinical, two digital — committing to a focused MVP with a fixed scope, a short timeline, and a low operating cost.

What we are asking for is the capital to execute the first step well. What we are offering is clinical integrity, structured execution, disciplined use of funds, and a long-term vision that places trust above growth.

We believe the first click a woman makes toward therapy should feel safe. This is the work we are prepared to do.
